This details LTT Anthonys experiance with PiKVM. PiKVM is a RasperryPi hat you can attach to a pi to turn it into a KVMoIP. It can allow you to interact with a computer (including the bios) remotley. ## [Writing at work](https://lcamtuf.substack.com/p/writing-at-work)
Fun fact, the pciture in this article is from Mad Men (a great TV show everyone should watch atleast once). This article is a gold mine for advice on writing. It is specifically geared for wtiting things down at work, but it also works for allot of this blog. The poitns boil down to three key ones

* The way you want something to read, and the way it actually does are not the same thing
* If it doesnt relate back the title, it doesnt belong in the post
* Write for your audiance, not for yourself - This article is after all for work, so while * If you are writing for an audiance get on their level - Anticipate questions, and dont bore with detail
* Keep it simple stupid - The post details two resorces for this: [The Economist Style Guide](https://www.amazon.com/Style-Guide-Economist-Books/dp/1610399811) and [The Chicago Manual of Style](https://www.amazon.com/Chicago-Manual-Style-17th/dp/022628705X).
* Address the correct issue to get the correct the solution - "Work harder" is not as useful as "we need to achieve x by y - we need to either reallocate resouece, or get more resources".
* If dealing with an argument find the underlying issue - find the facts that both agree on, else you are wating everyones time

With longer form text i.e. these posts he suggests the following four step process
1) Start with a rough draft - Nothing iron cald, just ideas in an order to get the idea o the flow
2) Begin fleshing out paragraphs
3) Edit this so it appicable for the read
4) Final tweaks

This is a bit tricky for me with thr reader part - This blog has always been for me. Truthfully I do not actually care of other people read it, much less understand it. My target audiance is me. Perhaps there is still seomthing to be learnt here however - I should write for my future self, as he is the one who is reading it.
